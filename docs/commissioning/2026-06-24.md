## Key finding: 7I97T isolated inputs are behind an InMux
`sudo mesaflash --device 7i97t --addr 10.10.10.10 --readhmid` (run with LinuxCNC
**closed** — it needs the Ethernet port; raw socket needs root) showed the firmware
reads the 16 isolated inputs through an **InMux** (Addr0-3 + Data0, internal pins),
and drives the 6 isolated outputs through an **SSR** block. They are NOT plain GPIO.

- Changed `configs/ned/ned.hal` load line: **`num_inmuxs=0` → `num_inmuxs=1`**.
- The old comment claiming `num_inmuxs=0` was REQUIRED (that InMux "grabs the
  sserial pins") was **wrong** — InMux uses internal pins; SSerial uses TB4-15..18.
  Verified: with `num_inmuxs=1` the 7I84 still enumerates fine.
- Cabinet inputs now appear as **`hm2_7i97.0.inmux.00.input-00..15`**
  (the inmux also exposes `enc0..3-count` for muxed MPGs).
- Isolated outputs (spare) are **`hm2_7i97.0.ssr.00.out-00..05`**.

`mesaflash --readhmid` is the authoritative, firmware-dependent pin map — the
source for terminal↔FPGA-function. `halcmd show pin` only gives driver gpio names.

### Design note — reliable "tool present" from sig-rack-pos
The rack prox nulls out over the drive dogs, so it's rotation-dependent. To use it
as a tool-present check, **spin the spindle and integrate the signal over ≥1 rev**:
treat "any TRUE during the spin" (or TRUE-fraction over a threshold) as tool present.
A single instantaneous read can false-negative when a dog is parked under the sensor.
